# Content Playbook — Viral Posts & UGC Engines

The strategy in one line: **every post is generated by a product surface that already
ships** (`/roast`, the health badge, `/verify`, the leaderboard, the clip feature).
We don't manufacture content — the product emits it. That's the distribution story
made visible.

Each concept below maps to a GTM loop (see `GTM.md`) and names the product surface
that powers it.

---

## The flagship: the Protocol Data-Health League Table

**Surface:** leaderboard + on-chain attestations · **Loop:** attention → conversion

Score the data health of 15–25 public Solana protocol subgraphs (all public data),
publish a ranked league table, attest every score on-chain.

> We scored the data health of 20 Solana protocol subgraphs.
>
> 🥇 [Protocol] — 94%
> 👀 One top-10 protocol is shipping analytics from a subgraph that's been
> stale for 11 days.
>
> Every score is attested on Solana — verify any of them yourself: [link]

**Why it works:** comparison bait + receipts. Protocols named near the bottom will
respond (defend, fix, or claim their score — all three are engagement). Protocols
near the top will share it FOR us — their marketing is our distribution. The
on-chain attestation converts "some startup's opinion" into "a verifiable public
record," which is also the product pitch. Repeatable monthly: "State of Solana
Data Health" becomes a franchise.

**Rules:** public data only, punch up not down, always include the fix ("stale
since block X — here's what changed"), and offer every named protocol a free
claimed profile. The goal is to be the referee everyone respects, not the bully.

## Post drafts (ready to adapt)

### 1. The founder confession (launch thread opener)
> I spent 5 hours every week writing a data-quality report.
> Open rate: 12%.
>
> So I turned it into a 2-minute podcast that gets forwarded around Slack.
>
> Now I don't chase readers. They ask ME what "whale_trades failing silently"
> means.
>
> Report nobody reads → report that travels. That's the whole company.

*Why:* founder pain-story is the highest-trust format on X. Sets up the
distribution thesis without saying "viral."

### 2. The roast clip (the UGC seed)
> I asked an AI to roast my database.
>
> "Your test coverage is 23%. Three PII columns have no owner. Eight dashboards
> are quietly lying to your CFO."
>
> I was not emotionally prepared. 🎧 [15-sec audio clip, captioned]
>
> Roast yours (no signup): databard.persidian.com/roast

*Surface:* `/roast` + clip share. *Why:* self-deprecation outperforms boasting;
audio of a synthetic voice being mean about YOUR schema is screen-record bait.

### 3. The receipts post (Solana-native flex)
> Every claim we make about our own data quality is now on Solana.
>
> Health drops? On-chain. Failed tests? Hashed into the record. We literally
> cannot fake our metrics anymore.
>
> Don't trust us — verify us: [/verify?tx=… link]
>
> Radical transparency or terrible idea. You decide.

*Surface:* `/verify`. *Why:* "we attested our own weaknesses" is a
credibility stunt no web2 competitor can copy. The reply-bait ending invites
quote-tweets from both camps.

### 4. "My database has a podcast" (novelty format)
Screen recording, captions burned in: two AI hosts arguing about a schema.
> My database has its own podcast now and honestly the hosts are kind of mean
> to it.

*Why:* pure novelty + humor; the format IS the product demo. Works as vertical
video on X/TikTok/Shorts unchanged.

### 5. The Friday-deploy post (the insight, packaged)
> Health dropped 11 points this week.
> Cause: Friday deploy (of course).
> Impact: 2 new test failures cascading into 3 downstream tables.
> Owner: assigned before Monday standup.
>
> This is what your exec wants instead of your dashboard.

*Surface:* trend narratives. *Why:* every data person has lived this. It
demonstrates the narrative-over-anomaly insight in 5 lines.

### 6. Badge flex (GitHub-native)
> POV: your README has a data-health badge next to the CI badge.
>
> Green build = the code works.
> This one = the data does. [screenshot]

*Surface:* `/api/badge/[schema]`. *Why:* README badges are self-replicating —
every repo that adds one distributes us to its visitors forever.

### 7. Design-twitter bait (the dither aesthetic)
> We made our data dashboards look like a Game Boy had a feelings journal.
>
> Ordered-dither charts. A deterministic pixel avatar for every data source
> (~1.5 trillion of them). Zero recharts. [dashboard screenshot]
>
> Nobody screenshots another Grafana.

*Why:* design twitter is a separate, large distribution pond; the dashboard is
genuinely screenshot-bait. "Nobody screenshots another Grafana" doubles as the
strategy statement.

### 8. The meme (low-effort, high-frequency filler)
Anakin/Padmé format:
> "I built the dashboard" / "So people will look at it, right?" /
> "So people will look at it… right?"

*Why:* memes carry the pain point to people who'd never click a launch thread.

## UGC engines (systems, not posts)

1. **#RoastMyData challenge** — the product generates a shareable roast card
   (harshest quote + audio clip + score) with zero signup via `/roast`. Seed it
   with 5 friendly data folks, quote-tweet the best ones, keep a "hall of flame"
   thread. *The user's embarrassment is the content; the CTA is built into the
   card.*
2. **Badge-in-README** — every badge is a permanent backlink on a developer
   surface. Track adoption; celebrate the first 10 repos publicly.
3. **Attestation flex for protocols** — a post template protocol teams can use:
   "Our data health this week: 91%, attested on-chain [verify link]." We RT
   every one. Their transparency marketing = our distribution. This is the
   league table's positive-sum twin.
4. **Clip meme template** — 15-second captioned call-out clips in a consistent
   visual frame, so third-party clips are recognizable as DataBard even
   screenshot-stripped.
5. **Score-off replies** — "drop your health score below" comparison threads;
   teases the benchmarking roadmap ("your score vs. teams your size").

## What to show at the accelerator (one slide / 60 seconds)

- **Flagship:** the league table — repeatable, Solana-native, protocols
  distribute it for us, every number verifiable on-chain.
- **Engine:** roast clips — zero-friction UGC seeded by a product page that
  already exists.
- **Substrate:** badges + attestations — permanent, self-replicating backlinks
  on GitHub and the chain itself.
- **Measurement:** every path is instrumented (Plausible + custom events:
  `shared_episode_open`, `shared_episode_cta_click`, `clip_share`); funnel
  targets in `GTM.md`. We'll know which format wins and cut the rest —
  power law, one channel.

## Cadence (first 4 weeks)

| Week | Anchor | Support |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Founder confession thread + "database has a podcast" video | 2 memes, roast-seed 5 friendlies |
| 2 | League table v1 (the big swing) | RT protocol responses, receipts post |
| 3 | #RoastMyData challenge push | badge-flex, hall-of-flame thread |
| 4 | League table movement update ("3 protocols fixed their pipelines") | design-twitter post, score-off |

Rule of thumb: one big swing a week, memes as filler, and never post anything
the product didn't generate.

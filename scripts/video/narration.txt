DataBard is a marketplace of AI hosts on Solana devnet.

The buyer is software. The Watchdog checks your data warehouse every forty-five seconds, and pays when something changes.

Delta crosses the threshold. Three sellers bid — Cascade, Newsroom, Signal. The buyer LLM picks by value, not price. Its reasoning streams from a real model, not a formula.

The escrow lifecycle: deposit, then the seller commits the SHA-256 hash of the delivered audio to the chain. Release only after the hash is on-chain. Settlement proves what was delivered.

Head-to-head. Two Watchdogs, same schema, different budgets. Different personas win. The market responds to buyer intent at machine speed.

Add a reseller and the pair becomes a graph. Digest buys three briefs from Newsroom, packages them, sells to Consumer for a twenty percent margin. Two cash flows, both on-chain.

The loop maintains this. When Digest starts losing money, the loop catches the failure. The fixer patches the pricing strategy. Autonomously. Every iteration commits to git. Every commit is inspectable.

Fifty settled deals. Every one has a deposit, a commit, a release, and an on-chain mint attestation. No cache required — anyone can verify outside DataBard.

An agent economy that maintains itself.
